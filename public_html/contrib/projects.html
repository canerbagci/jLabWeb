Current Projects

A Datawarehouse For The Analysis Of Micro-RNA Metabolic Network Control

This project brings together the facilities developed in jLab (pre-miRNA, mature miRNA, miRNA target prediction) with the tools developed in Bielefeld (VANESA, graph merging, visualization).

The integrated tool will enable the intersection of human miRNA and gene regulatory networks and their exploration.

Novel Human Proteins, Detection and Validation of Alternative Open Reading Frames

The dogma 1 gene -> 1 protein has long been abandoned and it is clear that a genomic locus can give rise to many proteins.

Here we try to find evidence for alternative start site usage and its efficiency using MS/MS data and computational prediction in concert.

Improving human gene annotation using mass spectrometric data from blood plasma

In this project, current annotation of human genome by exploting publicly available mass spectrometry data of human blood plasmasome is revised.

The automated analysis of genome annotation will be performed on a new proteogenomics workflow that is developed on KNIME Data Mining platform in the scope of this project.
